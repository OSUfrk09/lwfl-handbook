Fantasy Football in Plain English

Draft real NFL players to form a fantasy team. Each week, your starters earn points from their real‑game stats. You face another team head‑to‑head; highest score wins. Good records reach the playoffs, and each league’s champion battles in Week 17 for the Eternal Crown.

  • Snake Draft — order flips each round.
  • Waivers/FA — acquire players via claims or instant adds.
  • Lineups — start your best players weekly.
  • Trades — commish‑reviewed for fairness.
  • Half‑PPR — 0.5 points per catch.
  • Playoffs — 6 teams; top 2 byes; champions meet W17.

League Structure

  • Two leagues: Alpha & Omega
  • 10 teams per league
  • Draft order will be set by drawing numbers from a hat. If you are not available the commisioner will pick your number for you
  • Consolation bracket with Plunger Trophy (Toilet Bowl)
  • League winners: Genesis & Revelation trophies
  • Winners clash in Week 17 for the Eternal Crown

Draft Day Details & Prep

Before the Draft

  • Join your league and confirm the Draft Room opens on your device.
  • Build a Draft Queue (tiers by position) and pre-rank players.
  • Test Wi‑Fi or hotspot; keep a backup device handy.
  • If you might be late: fill a deep queue and enable Auto-Pick.

During the Draft

  • Mind the clock and the snake turnarounds.
  • Track positional runs; don’t panic—trust your tiers.
  • Use the mobile app if your laptop lags.

If You Can’t Make It

Heads up: Tell a commissioner. Pre-rank, build your queue, and toggle Auto-Pick so Sleeper follows your plan.

Starter Strategy

  • Balance byes so you’re not empty at one position.
  • Target upside for FLEX.
  • Consider a backup RB/WR with a clear path to volume.

Setting Your Lineup Each Week

Key Things to Watch

  • Game times vary: Thu night; Sun 1:00/4:05/4:25/8:20 ET; Mon night.
  • Roster locking: players lock at their game’s kickoff.
  • Thursday players: start them in position slots, not FLEX.
  • Check bye weeks and injuries early.

How-To

  • Mobile: League → Matchup or Team → tap a player → Start/Bench.
  • Web: League → Team → drag & drop between bench and starters.
Pro Tip: Do a midweek pass (after waivers) and a Sunday morning pass. Turn on notifications for late injury news.

Waivers, Free Agency & Adds/Drops

Definitions

  • Waivers: Claim period; managers submit claims that process at a set time. With rolling waivers, a successful claim moves you to the back of the order.
  • Free Agents (FA): Players not on waivers can be added instantly.

LWFL Rules

  • Type: Rolling waivers
  • Process Time: Tuesdays at 3:00 AM ET
  • After a Drop: Player sits on waivers for 2 days

Add/Drop How-To

  • Mobile: Players tab → search/filter → Add (FA) or Claim (waivers). If full, choose a drop.
  • Web: Players → filter by Available → Add/Claim → confirm drop if needed.
  • Submit multiple claims and reorder preferences; if #1 fails, Sleeper tries #2, etc.
Tip: After waivers run, unclaimed players become free agents until the next waiver lock.

IR Slot (Injured Reserve)

  • You have 1 IR spot. A player must have an eligible injury/out designation to move there.
  • Moving a player to IR frees a bench spot for an add.
  • When the player is no longer eligible, you must move them off IR before adding more players.
Check Status: Eligibility can change late in the week. Verify before kickoffs.

Bye Weeks (Plan Ahead)

  • Players on bye score 0 that week—bench them.
  • During the draft, avoid stacking key starters with the same bye.
  • During the season, look 2–3 weeks ahead and stash replacements early.
Tip: Sleeper player pages display BYE weeks clearly—use them to plan.

Trades

  • Commissioner review ensures fairness across skill levels.
  • Deadline: Week 11 (no trades after).
  • How to propose: Open another team → select offer/request players → add notes → send. They can accept/counter/decline.
  • Trade Block: Optionally list players you’re open to moving.
Fair Play: Seek mutual benefit. No collusion; suspicious trades can be vetoed and reversed.

Playoffs & Consolation

  • Playoff Field: 6 teams; top 2 seeds receive a first‑round bye.
  • Week 14: Playoffs begin for both leagues.
  • Week 17: Alpha champ vs Omega champ for the Eternal Crown.
  • League Trophies: Alpha → Genesis Trophy; Omega → Revelation Trophy.
  • Consolation Bracket: Last 4 teams in each league play week 14 & 15. Last place teams between both leagues battle for the Plunger Trophy in week 16.

Notifications

Enable push notifications for injuries, lineup reminders, waiver results, and trade offers—especially helpful for beginners.

  • Mobile: Profile → Settings → Notifications.
  • Web: Profile menu → Notifications.

League Chat

Use built‑in chat for announcements, quick questions, trade talk, and friendly banter. Keep it respectful and fun.

Streaming Defenses

Many managers “stream” D/ST: pick up a defense facing a weak offense each week rather than holding one all season. Example: a defense playing against a weak offense is often a better start than a mediocre defense against an elite QB.

Etiquette

  • Always set a lineup, even if your record dips.
  • Respond to trade offers politely and promptly.
  • Be active but fair on waivers—no griefing.

Glossary

  • ADP: Average Draft Position — where a player is typically picked.
  • Boom/Bust: High vs low scoring potential — high risk, high reward.
  • Ceiling/Floor: The likely best-case vs worst-case outcome for a player.
  • FLEX: A lineup spot that allows multiple positions (RB/WR/TE).
  • Handcuff: Backup RB who steps in if the starter is injured or rested.
  • PPR: Points Per Reception. Half-PPR = 0.5 pts per catch.
  • Proj.: Projected points — a prediction, not a guarantee.
  • Q: “Questionable” injury tag — player may not play or start.
  • Stream: Swapping out position players (often K/DST) weekly based on matchups.
  • Tilt: Letting emotions lead to bad decisions. Happens. Don’t chase it.
  • Waivers: Time-delayed claim process for dropped or undrafted players.
